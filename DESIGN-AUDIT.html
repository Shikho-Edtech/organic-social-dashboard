Independent Design & UX Audit · 2026-04-18

Organic Social Dashboard

Heuristic evaluation, visual design review, accessibility assessment, and prioritized recommendations roadmap for web + mobile, pre- and post-login.

This is an internal analytics tool that has been iterated on thoughtfully and is well past scaffolding. The mobile-first rigor, the kind taxonomy (observed / ai / derived), the explicit sample-size badges, the adaptive min-N gating, and the tap-toggle InfoTooltip all show a team that takes data honesty seriously.

What it lacks is the polish layer that would make it feel like a product rather than a competent internal dashboard. The visual system is functional but generic Tailwind slate-gray; the brand palette exists but only surfaces on login, the header, and a handful of KPI accents. Information architecture has eight pages for what is effectively one data source, with real overlap between Engagement / Timing / Strategy. Accessibility is below WCAG AA for at least six common text treatments.

The dashboard is trustworthy and functional. It is not yet opinionated.

Top 5 strengths & opportunities

Strengths

  1. Data provenance is treated as a first-class design element (kind prop, DataFooter, sample-size badges, definition tooltips on every chart). This is rare and good.
  2. Mobile-first rules are real, not aspirational. Evidence of actual iteration after desktop-only regressions.
  3. Plan page progressive disclosure (<details> per day with format chips peeking) is genuinely good UX. Native semantics, respects density.
  4. Adaptive min-N gating on Timing and Engagement is intellectually honest. Prevents the "one viral dawn post wins Early" trap.
  5. DateRangePicker and Explore filter toolbar are both clean, keyboard-dismissible, viewport-clamped. Better than most SaaS equivalents.

Biggest opportunities

  1. Accessibility below WCAG AA in 40+ places. text-slate-400 on white (2.85:1) used for real content. Focus rings missing from most interactive elements.
  2. No loading or skeleton states. Cold loads look broken for 300-1500ms.
  3. Brand palette is under-deployed. Dashboard feels like shadcn, not Shikho.
  4. 8 pages is too many. Engagement / Timing / Strategy overlap. A 5-page IA would reduce cognitive load.
  5. Strategy Weekly Verdict hero is visually underbuilt for what's literally the most important content on the dashboard.

Visual design & aesthetics · 6 findings
MajorM effortV1 — Brand palette is present but invisible in-product
Whattailwind.config.ts:18-23 defines brand-shikho-indigo/blue/pink/orange. They're used on login, Nav, a few Explore KPI texts, and Plan day gradients. The dashboard itself uses brand-cyan/green/amber/purple (generic) or slate. A user lands on Overview and sees zero Shikho branding except the top-left logo.
WhyThe team invested in a brand identity and the dashboard doesn't cash in. Competing dashboards (Buffer, Meta Business Suite) have a distinct signature color for primary data — this one picks cyan.
FixPick brand-shikho-indigo as the primary data color (reach trend, primary bars) and brand-shikho-pink as the accent. Reorder the PALETTE arrays in BarChart.tsx:23 and Donut.tsx:4.
MajorS effortV2 — Typography hierarchy compresses at the bottom
WhatFive distinct sizes below 14px: text-xs, text-[11px], text-[10px], text-[9px], and text-sm used adjacent. Eye can't establish hierarchy.
WhyVisual noise; 9-10px fails AA contrast thresholds more aggressively.
FixCollapse to three small sizes: text-xs (12px) for labels, text-[11px] for eyebrow/uppercase. Drop text-[10px] and text-[9px] entirely. If a label doesn't fit at 11px it's too verbose.
MinorS effortV3 — Kind badges compete with sample-size badges in ChartCard header
WhatCard.tsx:51-68 has title + (i) + sample-size + kind all flex-wrapping on a narrow card. Four things compete for one row. Kind badge is smaller than sample-size badge — wrong visual weight.
FixKind is already a left-border treatment. Remove the redundant kind text badge. Document kinds once in DataFooter.
PolishS effortV4 — Inline SVGs inconsistent in stroke-width and size
WhatStroke widths range from 1.5 to 3.5 across the codebase. Sizes: 10/11/12/13/14/16 px all in play. Icons look hand-drawn, not designed.
FixStandardize: strokeWidth="2" ornamental (chevrons, info), strokeWidth="2.5" affordance (close, check-when-selected). Three sizes: 12, 14, 16.
MinorS effortV5 — Spacing rhythm breaks between KPI strips and chart grids
WhatKPI rows use mb-6, chart grids mb-4, some have none. Gaps inconsistent as you scroll.
FixPage wrapper uses space-y-6. Remove per-grid margin-bottoms.
PolishM effortV6 — Cards have no visual family
WhatEvery card is bg-white border border-slate-200 rounded-xl p-6. No shadow, no hover state, no subtle gradient. Flatness reads as "not cared about".
FixAdd shadow-sm baseline. hover:shadow-md hover:-translate-y-px for clickable cards only. Consider bg-gradient-to-br from-white to-slate-50/40 for KPI cards.
UX heuristics (Nielsen's 10) · 7 findings
MajorM effortU1 — No loading states anywhere
WhatAll 8 pages are Server Components with revalidate = 300. No loading.tsx, no skeletons, no Suspense.
WhyNielsen #1 (visibility of system status). Cold load = blank white for 300-1500ms. Feels broken on slow mobile networks.
FixAdd app/loading.tsx with skeleton KPI cards + skeleton chart block. Next.js renders it automatically.
MajorS effortU2 — Error recovery is absent
WhatNo error.tsx files. If getPosts() throws, user sees Next.js's default "Application error" screen. Already happened 2026-04-17 per CHANGELOG.
FixAdd app/error.tsx with friendly card, "Try again" button calling reset(), tech hint about 5-min cache.
MinorS effortU3 — Nav active state has no Shikho accent on desktop
WhatNav.tsx:81 — active tab is border-slate-900 text-slate-900 (neutral). Mobile correctly uses brand indigo. Desktop is inconsistent.
FixOne-line change to border-brand-shikho-indigo text-brand-shikho-indigo.
MinorS effortU4 — "Rendered {timestamp}" appears on every page except Plan
WhatPlan passes showPicker={false} which hides the entire right column — including the render timestamp. Yet Plan caches the same way.
WhyPlan is EXACTLY where staleness matters most. Pipeline runs Monday 10am BDT.
FixSplit render timestamp from the picker column. Show it even without picker. Or give Plan its own "Generated {date}" chip.
MinorS effortU5 — "Best X" KPIs use color but the color isn't informative
WhatBest Format=cyan, Pillar=pink, Hook=green, Spotlight=purple. Rotates purely for visual variety, not meaning.
WhyYour own CLAUDE.md: "When categorizing items, use color to distinguish things — not just labels." Inverse also applies: color not conveying meaning is decoration masquerading as data.
FixEither (a) all "Best X" in shikho-indigo (unified best-of feel), or (b) tie color to what the value represents — Best Format=Reel should pull the pink that Reels uses on the Plan page.
MinorS effortU6 — Disclosure chevron affordances inconsistent
WhatPlan uses > → v. Strategy findings use v → ^. Strategy verdict uses >. Three patterns on related page types.
FixStandardize: all <details> summaries use down-chevron rotating 180° on open.
MinorS effortU7 — Strategy Weekly Verdict's expand affordance is weak
WhatTiny 10px "more" label at top-right that goes slate-400 → slate-600 on open. On a hero card, this is underwhelming.
FixVisible "Read full verdict →" ghost button at bottom-right. Converts to "Collapse ↑" on open. Make the affordance the primary CTA of the section.
Information architecture · 3 findings
MajorL effortIA1 — 8 pages is too many for this data set
WhatTrends (reach over time) overlaps Overview's reach trend. Engagement (rate cuts) and Timing (rate cuts by day/hour) are both rate-cuts of the same posts. Strategy and Plan both consume weekly pipeline output.
WhyA user asking "how is pillar X performing" checks Engagement, Overview, Explore, and sometimes Strategy. Answer lives in four places.
FixConsider a 5-page IA: Overview · Content (Engagement + Trends merged) · Timing · Reels · Strategy (Plan merged in as "Next week" section). Explore stays as a power-user drill-down. Even just merging Plan into Strategy is M and reduces the top-bar.
MinorS effortIA2 — Top-to-bottom page flow is inconsistent
WhatOverview = KPIs → 2x2 grid. Engagement = Best X → 2x2 → 1x1 → 2x2. Strategy = verdict → 1x2 funnel → findings → top/under → watch-outs. No unifying pattern.
FixEstablish a page template: header → headline KPIs (up to 4-5) → primary chart (full width or 2-up) → secondary charts → detail tables/rows.
MinorS effortIA3 — Plan has no date for "next week"
WhatSubtitle says "Next week's content calendar" but only per-slot dates are shown. If pipeline hasn't run, "next week" becomes "stale week" invisibly.
FixShow week range in subtitle ("Week of Monday 21 Apr — Sunday 27 Apr · generated Mon 14 Apr 10:03 BDT"). Pull from slot.date min/max.

Accessibility · 7 findings
CriticalM effortA1 — text-slate-400 for real content (42 files)
WhatUsed for sample-size badges, KPI sublabels, render timestamps, week-ending dates, Best X reliability labels, Explore post meta rows. All real informational content. 2.85:1 on white fails WCAG AA (needs 4.5:1).
WhyOn mobile brightness auto-dimmed in daylight, slate-400 is effectively unreadable.
FixGlobal replace: text-slate-400 → text-slate-500 (4.58:1) for all content with real meaning. Reserve slate-400 for ornamental separators like ·.
CriticalS effortA2 — No visible focus rings on most interactive elements
WhatOnly 3 files have focus: classes, all for input fields. Nav buttons, MultiSelects, disclosure summaries, sign-out — no focus rings.
WhyWCAG 2.4.7 fail. Keyboard-only users lose their place.
FixSingle base-layer rule in globals.css:
button:focus-visible, a:focus-visible, summary:focus-visible { outline: 2px solid theme('colors.brand.shikho-indigo'); outline-offset: 2px; }
MajorS effortA3 — InfoTooltip not keyboard accessible
WhatOpens on click, closes on outside mousedown. No Escape-to-close. No aria-describedby linking tooltip text to button.
FixGive tooltip an id. Use aria-describedby={tooltipId}. Add Escape keydown handler. Has role="tooltip" already.
MinorS effortA4 — DateRangePicker button has no accessible name
Fixaria-label={`Change date range, current: ${displayLabel}`} + aria-expanded={open} + aria-haspopup="dialog".
MinorL effortA5 — Recharts tooltips not keyboard reachable
WhatHover-only. Data in charts inaccessible via keyboard.
FixFor internal tool, document as known gap and provide a "View data" button expanding a table. Defer to L.
MinorS effortA6 — list-none disclosures lose triangle without replacement focus
FixBundled with A2 — focus ring fixes this automatically.
MinorM effortA7 — Pixel-pinned sizes don't scale with user font-size preference
Whatmin-h-[18px] etc. won't scale at 200% zoom (WCAG 1.4.4).
FixUse em-based units where possible. Lower priority.

Performance · 3 findings
MinorM effortP1 — No streaming, no Suspense boundaries
WhatEach page awaits all data upfront. Nothing renders until slowest call returns.
FixSplit KPI bar (fast) from chart grid (heavier). Suspense boundary at chart-grid level with skeleton fallback.
MinorM effortP2 — "Rendered timestamp" is request-time, not data-fetch-time
WhatShows when request arrived, not when Sheets was queried. Conflicts with DataFooter's 5-min cache.
FixRename to "Data as of {timestamp}" using actual Sheets fetch time.
PolishS effortP3 — Reels page parses retention curves redundantly
WhatOuter 60-second loop × inner reels loop × parseRetentionCurve call = 3000 JSON parses for 50 reels.
FixParse each reel's curve once, cache in local array, iterate seconds inside.

Things that are fine — do not touch

  • DataFooter — the pattern and content are both good.
  • InfoTooltip implementation — tap-toggle + hover pairing is right. Only add the a11y improvements in A3.
  • Plan day-card progressive disclosure — polished and appropriate.
  • Explore filter toolbar — best-designed component in the app.
  • Adaptive MIN_N and the 95% CI lower-bound ranking — intellectually correct.
  • kind taxonomy (observed/ai/derived) — valuable data literacy. Only simplify the visual presentation (recommendation #8).

Bottom line. The dashboard is structurally sound and statistically honest — these are the hard parts to get right and they are right. The remaining work is almost entirely polish and accessibility: fix contrast, add loading/error states, deploy the brand palette consistently, trim the 8-page IA, and rebuild the Strategy verdict hero to match its importance. Priorities 1-10 are all Small-effort and would visibly lift quality in under a day of work. Priorities 11-18 are the craft pass. Priorities 19-20 are the strategic rebuild.
